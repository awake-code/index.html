    The Crime Paradox

    An alarming rise in politically motivated threats occurs alongside a crucial turning point: overall violent crime is decreasing significantly, with the national homicide rate falling about 15% in 2024, reaching near-generational lows not seen since the early 1960s. Chicago follows a similar decline, with violent crime in Chicago continues to fall, homicides lowest in a decade, police data shows.     Inspiration

    The creation of this website stems from the need to understand the dynamics of crime in major urban centers. By comparing New York City and Chicago, we aim to uncover patterns, trends, and factors that contribute to crime rates in these cities with the goal of informing policy decisions and community initiatives aimed at reducing crime and improving public safety, all while analyzing how crime rates how truly reduced throughout the nation in crime-ridden cities.

    • Overall Chicago Crime Has Fallen By Remarkable Numbers
      Chicago led the nation in overall violent crime reduction during the first nine months of 2025 with a 22.1% drop in violent crime. In the first half of 2025 Chicago experienced a 33% reduction in homicides, doubling the nationwide average.
