    Theft Percentage Comparison between NYC and Chicago

    The large distance between the two points on the graph emphasizes the vast difference between theft as a share of the crime in Chicago and theft as a share of the crime in New York. This is essential because when researchers study and work to build effective crime policy, it is crucial not to generalize for cities. As shown by data, different cities have different needs. This also points to a trend that Chicago might have a theft-specific problem and weak policy on the issue, when compared to New York, a city who in theory should have similar statistical percentages.
